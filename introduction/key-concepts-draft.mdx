Before diving into Commenda's features, it helps to understand the key terms and concepts you'll encounter throughout the platform.

## Company

A **company** is your top-level account in Commenda. It represents the parent organization that owns or manages one or more business entities. All your team members, settings, and billing are managed at the company level.

## Entity

An **entity** (also called a corporation) is an individual legal business — such as a C-Corp, LLC, Private Limited company, or LLP. A single company in Commenda can manage dozens or even hundreds of entities across different countries and jurisdictions.

Each entity has its own:
- Business information (name, EIN, formation date)
- Key persons (directors, officers, signatories)
- Ownership structure (stakeholders, cap table)
- Registrations (tax IDs, state registrations)
- Documents

## Compliance item

A **compliance item** is a specific obligation your entity needs to fulfill — such as filing an annual report, paying franchise tax, or submitting a tax return. Commenda automatically generates compliance items based on your entity's type, jurisdiction, and registration status.

Each compliance item has a due date, status, and can be linked to a service request if you need help completing it.

## Service request

A **service request** is a unit of work being done on your behalf — like incorporating a new entity, filing a tax return, or registering for sales tax. Service requests track tasks, assignees, deadlines, and related documents.

Service requests can be:
- Created manually by your team
- Generated from the service marketplace
- Created automatically from compliance items

## Task

A **task** is an individual action item within a service request. Tasks are assigned to specific people (you, your advisor, or a Commenda agent) and tracked through completion.

Tasks have statuses like **Not started**, **In progress**, **Action required**, and **Completed**.

## Key person

A **key person** is a director, officer, signatory, or other individual associated with one of your entities. Key persons are managed centrally, so the same individual can be linked to multiple entities without duplicating their information.

## Registration

A **registration** is a record of your entity being registered with a government authority — such as a state tax registration, business license, or employer ID. Registrations track the registration number, status, filing frequency, and associated credentials.

## Jurisdiction

A **jurisdiction** is the specific state, province, or administrative region where an entity is registered or has obligations. For example, a US company might have entities registered in Delaware, California, and New York — each is a separate jurisdiction with its own compliance requirements.

## Advisor firm

An **advisor firm** (also called an accountant firm or CA firm) is an external accounting or advisory firm that works with your company through Commenda. Advisor firms can view your entities, manage compliance work, and deliver services on your behalf.

## Agent firm

An **agent firm** is a service provider that handles specific tasks like incorporation filings, registered agent services, or tax registrations. Agent firms work through the Commenda agent portal to deliver services assigned to them.

## Integration

An **integration** is a connection between Commenda and an external system — like your accounting software (QuickBooks, Xero, etc.) or bank account (via Plaid). Integrations automatically sync data so you don't need to enter it manually.
