Use the bank accounts section to store and manage banking information for your business entities. You can add multiple bank accounts, set a primary account, and update account details as needed.

## Add a bank account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to your entity">
    Go to **Entities** in the sidebar and select the entity you want to add a bank account to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open bank accounts">
    Click the **Bank Accounts** tab, then click **Add Bank Account**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter bank details">
    Fill in the required information:
    - **Bank name** — the full name of your financial institution
    - **Account holder name** — the legal name on the account (should match your entity name)
    - **Account number** — your full bank account number
    - **Routing number** — your bank's routing number (9 digits for US banks)
    - **IFSC code** — for Indian bank accounts
    - **Account type** — checking, savings, current, money market, or escrow
    - **Currency** — the account currency (USD, EUR, INR, etc.)
    - **Set as primary** — check this box if this should be your default account
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Review all information for accuracy and click **Save**. The account will appear in your list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Double-check your account and routing numbers before saving. Incorrect numbers can cause payment failures.
</Tip>

### Connecting via Plaid

If you prefer to connect your bank account automatically, Commenda integrates with Plaid. When available, you'll be guided through Plaid's secure connection flow — search for your bank, sign in with your banking credentials, and select which accounts to connect. Plaid handles the authentication securely, and Commenda never stores your banking login credentials.

## Update a bank account

1. Navigate to your entity's **Bank Accounts** tab.
2. Click the **Edit** button (pencil icon) next to the account.
3. Update the bank name, account holder name, account type, currency, or primary status.
4. Click **Save Changes**.

<Warning>
You cannot edit the account number or routing number after creation. If these need to change, add a new account with the correct information and delete the old one.
</Warning>

## Set a primary account

Your primary account is used as the default for transactions and payments.

1. Navigate to the **Bank Accounts** tab for your entity.
2. Click **Set as Primary** on the account you want, or edit the account and check the **Set as primary** box.

<Info>
Only one account can be primary at a time. Setting a new primary account will automatically remove the primary status from the previous account.
</Info>

## View account details

Click any account to view its details:
- Bank name and account holder name
- Masked account number (last 4 digits visible)
- Routing number
- Account type and currency
- Primary status and date added

<Note>
For security, account numbers are masked and only show the last 4 digits in the dashboard. The full number is securely stored and used for transactions.
</Note>

## Remove a bank account

<Warning>
Deleting a bank account is permanent and cannot be undone.
</Warning>

1. Navigate to the **Bank Accounts** tab.
2. Click the **Delete** button (trash icon) next to the account.
3. Confirm the deletion.

<Tip>
You cannot delete a primary account if it's the only account. Either add another account first, or remove the primary status before deleting.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

### I can't save my bank account

Check that all required fields are filled and formatted correctly. Common causes: missing required fields, invalid routing number format (must be 9 digits for US banks), or invalid characters in the account number.

### I need to change my account number

Account and routing numbers cannot be edited after creation. Add a new bank account with the correct information, set it as primary if needed, then delete the old account.

### The primary account won't change

Check that you have permission to edit bank accounts, the new account is fully saved, and you're clicking **Save** after making changes.
